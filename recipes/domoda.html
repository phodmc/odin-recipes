Domoda

Description

Peanut stew or groundnut stew, also known as maafe, sauce d'arachide or tigadèguèna is a stew that is a staple food in Western Africa. It originates from the Mandinka and Bambara people of Mali.

It is also known as `Domoda` in The Gambia

Ingredients

  • beef steak or chicken breast
  • large onion diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • cloves garlic minced
  • Roma tomatoes diced
  • 1/2 can 3 oz tomato paste

Steps

  1. In a Dutch oven, add water and cook stewing beef over medium-low heat until no longer pink.
  2. Add salt, garlic powder, and Maggi seasoning.
  3. Remove habeneros from stew.
  4. In a skillet, fry chicken legs. Remove meat from bones after frying.
  5. In a skillet, fry chicken legs. Remove meat from bones after frying.
  6. Serve over rice or with fufu.
